Ad Budget Optimization
Under Privacy Constraints

Meta-Signal is a reinforcement learning environment that simulates the real-world challenge of optimizing ad spend when signal is noisy, privacy budgets are limited, and regulators can suspend your campaigns mid-flight.

Why This Matters

📉

Meta's $232B Crisis

In Q3 2022, Apple's ATT prompt let users opt out of cross-app tracking. Meta lost 25% of its ad signal overnight — wiping $232B in market cap in a single day. This is the exact problem we simulate.

🔐

Differential Privacy

Real ad systems can't share exact conversion data. We model ε-differential privacy with Laplace noise — the same mechanism Meta uses in AEM (Aggregated Event Measurement).

🤖

RL Over Heuristics

Rule-based budget allocation breaks under noise. A trained RL agent learns to infer signal from noisy observations — directly applicable to Meta's Advantage+ signal recovery pipeline.

⚖️

Regulatory Reality

GDPR Art. 17 and CCPA create real operational risk. Task 4 simulates adversarial regulators that can suspend campaigns mid-episode, testing how quickly agents adapt.

🔒 Privacy Regimes — What You'll Encounter
As epsilon depletes, data quality degrades through four regimes. Your strategy must adapt in real time.
Standard Clean signal, minimal noise. Conversions are reliable estimates.
High Noise Laplace noise elevated. Use confidence intervals — don't trust point estimates.
Minimal Data Data minimisation enforced. Only 1 feature allowed. Be conservative.
Exhausted Epsilon depleted. Signal is near-random. Maintain allocation from last good step.
